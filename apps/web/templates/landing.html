The architecture of long-form listening

Cadence is designed for material that should not lose its shape just because it becomes audible.

The current sentence settles into a quiet field while the active word becomes the exact point of attention. This lets the eye stay with the voice instead of chasing it.

Playback, offsets, and layout all move together so the reading experience feels measured rather than mechanical.

Signal path

A simple system with deliberate boundaries.

  • Visible text is mapped in the frontend.
  • The backend streams Edge TTS audio and timing metadata.
  • Highlights are reconstructed from exact offsets, not guesswork.
  • Voice and speed changes restart from the live reading position.

Method

Quiet surfaces. Precise playback. Fewer broken lines of attention.

01

Respect the source

Rendering happens in the browser so the reader keeps the actual visible structure instead of flattening everything too early.

02

Stream, do not wait

Playback begins quickly because audio and timing metadata are streamed instead of forcing the user to wait for a full generation pass.

03

Map before you highlight

The system builds canonical text offsets first, then uses those offsets to recover sentence and word ranges for the UI.

04

Keep controls nearby

The web reader, popup, and floating extension controller are all designed to reduce travel between the text and the playback action.
